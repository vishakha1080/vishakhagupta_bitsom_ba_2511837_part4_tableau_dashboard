**Chart Selection Justification**

**1\. KPI Cards (Total Sales, Total Profit, Total Orders)**

**Question Answered**

What is the overall business performance?

**Why This Chart Type**

KPI cards provide immediate visibility into the most important metrics.

**Fields Used**

- Sales
- Profit
- Order ID

**Design Principle Applied**

Visual hierarchy and simplicity.

**Mistake Avoided**

Avoided cluttering the dashboard with unnecessary detail.

**2\. Sales Trends View (Line Chart)**

**Question Answered**

How has sales performance changed over time?

**Why This Chart Type**

Line charts are ideal for displaying trends and patterns across time periods.

**Fields Used**

- Order Date
- Sales

**Design Principle Applied**

Trend visualization and temporal analysis.

**Mistake Avoided**

Avoided using bar charts for long time-series data.

**3\. Regional Performance View (Bar Chart)**

**Question Answered**

Which regions contribute the most sales and profit?

**Why This Chart Type**

Bar charts allow quick comparison across categories.

**Fields Used**

- Region
- Sales
- Profit

**Design Principle Applied**

Comparability and ranking.

**Mistake Avoided**

Avoided pie charts, which make precise comparison difficult.

**4\. Category Profitability View (Bar Chart)**

**Question Answered**

Which product categories generate the highest profits?

**Why This Chart Type**

Supports direct comparison of profitability across categories.

**Fields Used**

- Category
- Sub-Category
- Profit

**Design Principle Applied**

Comparative analysis.

**Mistake Avoided**

Avoided excessive color variation that could distract users.

**5\. Customer Segment View (Bar Chart)**

**Question Answered**

Which customer segments drive business performance?

**Why This Chart Type**

Clearly compares sales and profit contribution across segments.

**Fields Used**

- Customer Segment
- Sales
- Profit

**Design Principle Applied**

Business segmentation analysis.

**Mistake Avoided**

Avoided presenting segment performance in separate charts.

**6\. Discount vs Profit View (Scatter Plot)**

**Question Answered**

How do discounts impact profitability?

**Why This Chart Type**

Scatter plots reveal relationships and correlations between variables.

**Fields Used**

- Discount
- Profit
- Product Name

**Design Principle Applied**

Relationship analysis.

**Mistake Avoided**

Avoided line charts that may imply a sequential relationship.

**7\. Return Analysis View (Bar Chart)**

**Question Answered**

How many orders are returned and where?

**Why This Chart Type**

Provides clear comparison between returned and non-returned orders.

**Fields Used**

- Return Flag
- Order ID

**Design Principle Applied**

Operational performance monitoring.

**Mistake Avoided**

Avoided using percentages without showing actual volume.

**8\. Shipping Performance View (Bar Chart)**

**Question Answered**

How efficiently are orders being delivered?

**Why This Chart Type**

Allows comparison of delivery performance across shipping modes.

**Fields Used**

- Ship Mode
- Delivery Days
- Order Count

**Design Principle Applied**

Operational efficiency analysis.

**Mistake Avoided**

Avoided displaying raw records that would reduce readability.

**9\. Profit Margin View**

**Question Answered**

Which regions generate the strongest margins?

**Why This Chart Type**

Highlights profitability quality rather than revenue volume.

**Fields Used**

- Profit Margin (Calculated Field)
- Region

**Design Principle Applied**

Focus on business value rather than activity volume.

**Mistake Avoided**

Avoided evaluating performance solely through sales metrics.
