# Executive Business Performance Dashboard

## Business Problem Summary

Organizations often struggle to understand how sales performance, profitability, customer behavior, returns, and operational efficiency interact to influence overall business results. This dashboard was created to provide leadership with a unified view of key performance indicators and support data-driven decision-making.

---

## Dataset Description

The dataset contains transactional business data including:

* Order ID
* Order Date
* Sales
* Profit
* Category
* Sub-Category
* Customer Segment
* Region
* Ship Mode
* Discount
* Return Status

The data was analyzed to identify trends, profitability drivers, operational risks, and growth opportunities.

---

## Tableau Workbook Description

The Tableau workbook provides an executive-level performance dashboard that combines financial, customer, product, and operational insights into a single interactive reporting environment.

The dashboard enables users to explore performance through filters and interactive visualizations.

---

## Calculated Fields Created

### Profit Margin

Profit Margin = Profit / Sales

### Delivery Days

Delivery Days = Ship Date - Order Date

Additional calculated measures were created to support KPI reporting and operational analysis.

## Key Business Insights

* Revenue performance remains strong across major business segments.
* Profitability varies significantly across categories and regions.
* Higher discount levels often reduce profit margins.
* Product returns create revenue leakage and operational inefficiencies.
* Customer segments contribute differently to sales and profit generation.
* Shipping performance directly impacts customer experience.

---

## Dashboard Story Summary

The dashboard demonstrates that revenue growth alone is insufficient for long-term success. While sales performance is strong, profitability is influenced by discounting strategies, return rates, category mix, and operational efficiency.

The findings suggest that leadership should focus on profit optimization, return reduction, and operational improvements alongside revenue growth initiatives.

---

## Assumptions and Limitations

### Assumptions

* Source data is accurate and complete.
* Profit calculations accurately reflect business performance.
* Return status is consistently recorded.

### Limitations

* Historical analysis only.
* No predictive forecasting included.
* External market conditions are not represented.
* Customer lifetime value metrics are unavailable.
